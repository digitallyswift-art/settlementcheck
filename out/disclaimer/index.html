Important: This is not legal advice

The calculator on SettlementCheck.co.uk provides estimates only. You must obtain independent legal advice from a qualified solicitor before signing a settlement agreement.

Calculator Disclaimer

What the calculator does

The SettlementCheck calculator applies the UK statutory redundancy formula (using 2024/25 rates, including a weekly pay cap of £643) and general market data to produce an estimate of the minimum statutory entitlement and typical settlement range for your situation.

What the calculator does not do

The calculator does not account for your specific employment contract, any enhanced redundancy terms, your negotiation history, PILON (payment in lieu of notice) clauses, gardening leave arrangements, bonus or share entitlements, restrictive covenants, or the strength of any potential discrimination or unfair dismissal claim.

Discrimination claims

If you have indicated potential discrimination in your situation, be aware that compensation for discrimination is uncapped in Employment Tribunals. The calculator cannot quantify discrimination claims — only a qualified solicitor can assess these.

Statutory rates

Statutory rates change annually. The calculator uses rates effective from April 2024. If you are reading this after April 2025, rates may have changed. Always confirm current rates with a solicitor.

No solicitor-client relationship

Using this calculator does not create a solicitor-client relationship. SettlementCheck is not regulated by the Solicitors Regulation Authority. We are an introduction service only.

Legal requirement for advice

Under Section 203 of the Employment Rights Act 1996, a settlement agreement (formerly a compromise agreement) is only legally binding if you have received independent legal advice from a qualified solicitor on the terms and effect of the agreement. A calculator estimate does not satisfy this requirement.
